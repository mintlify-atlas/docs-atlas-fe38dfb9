Custody Chains (Cadenas de Custodia) document the collection, transport, and disposition of wastewater and waste materials from project sites. These records are critical for compliance, billing, and environmental tracking.

## What is a Custody Chain?

A Custody Chain is a legal document that records:

- **Activity Details**: Date, time, location, and duration of waste collection
- **Volume Collected**: Gallons, barrels, and cubic meters of different waste types
- **Waste Types**: Black water, grey water, clean water, sludge, grease, treated wastewater
- **Personnel**: Technical personnel, drivers, and client contacts
- **Equipment Used**: Vehicles and equipment involved in collection
- **Signatures**: Chain of custody with contact person and transporter signatures

## Understanding Custody Chain Status

Custody chains progress through these statuses:

- **DRAFT** (Borrador): Being created or edited
- **CLOSE** (Cerrado): Finalized and included in work order sheet
- **CANCELLED** (Cancelado): Cancelled, not used
- **INVOICE** (Facturado): Billed to client via work order

<Note>
  You cannot delete a custody chain that has status CLOSE. This preserves the legal chain of custody.
</Note>

## Creating a Custody Chain

<Steps>
  <Step title="Navigate to Work Order Sheet">
    Select the work order sheet (planilla) where this custody chain will be included.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Click Create Custody Chain">
    Select "New Custody Chain" or "Add Custody Chain".
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Enter Basic Information">
    Fill in the activity details:
    
    **Automatic Fields:**
    - **Consecutive**: Auto-generated 7-digit number (e.g., 0000123)
    - **Sheet Project**: Linked to current work order
    
    **Date and Time:**
    - **Activity Date**: Date waste collection occurred
    - **Issue Date**: Date the document is created
    - **Start Time**: When collection began
    - **End Time**: When collection ended
    - **Time Duration**: Automatically calculated total hours
    
    **Location:**
    - **Location**: Specific site or camp location
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Assign Personnel and Equipment">
    Select the resources involved:
    
    **Technical Personnel:**
    - Choose the responsible technician from your team
    
    **Vehicle:**
    - Select the vacuum truck or transport vehicle used
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Specify Waste Types Collected">
    Check all applicable waste types:
    
    - **Black Water** (Aguas Negras): Sewage and toilet waste
    - **Grey Water** (Aguas Grises): Sink and shower wastewater
    - **Clean Water** (Aguas Limpias): Non-contaminated water
    - **Activated Sludge** (Lodos Activados): Treatment plant sludge
    - **Treated Wastewater** (Aguas Residuales Tratadas): Processed effluent
    - **Organic Grease** (Grasa Orgánica): Grease trap contents
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Enter Volume Totals">
    Record the quantities collected:
    
    - **Total Gallons**: Volume in gallons
    - **Total Barrels**: Volume in barrels (if applicable)
    - **Total Cubic Meters**: Volume in cubic meters
    
    <Tip>
      You can enter volumes in any or all units. The system tracks all three separately for different reporting needs.
    </Tip>
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Configure Logistics">
    Indicate if logistics services were provided:
    
    - **SI**: Logistics were performed
    - **NO**: No logistics
    - **NA**: Not applicable
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Save as Draft">
    Click Save. The custody chain is created with status DRAFT.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Adding Equipment Details

After creating the custody chain, add the equipment used:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Access Custody Chain Details">
    Open the custody chain you just created.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Add Equipment Line Item">
    Click "Add Equipment" or "Add Detail".
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Select Project Resource">
    Choose from resources assigned to the project:
    - Treatment plants
    - Tanks
    - Vacuum trucks
    - Related equipment
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Enter Equipment Description">
    Provide:
    - **Equipment**: Type or name (e.g., "BT LV OT")
    - **Code Equipment**: Detailed equipment identifier or notes
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Save Equipment Detail">
    The equipment is linked to this custody chain.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  Multiple equipment items can be added to a single custody chain to document all resources used during the collection activity.
</Note>

## Completing the Custody Chain

### Adding Contact Information

Document the client representative who received the service:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Client Contact Section">
    Enter information for the person who witnessed/approved the collection:
    
    - **Contact Name**: Full name of client representative
    - **DNI Contact**: ID card number
    - **Contact Position**: Job title or role
    - **Date Contact**: Date of signature
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Driver/Transporter Section">
    Enter driver information:
    
    - **Driver Name**: Name of the driver/operator
    - **DNI Driver**: Driver's ID number
    - **Driver Position**: Role (e.g., "Operator", "Driver")
    - **Driver Date**: Date of signature
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Uploading Documentation

<Steps>
  <Step title="Generate PDF">
    Create the custody chain PDF document with all information and signatures.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Upload File">
    Click "Upload" in the custody chain file section.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Select PDF">
    Choose the signed custody chain PDF (validated to be PDF format only).
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Confirm Upload">
    The file is stored and linked to the custody chain record.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
  Only PDF files are accepted for custody chain documentation to ensure consistency and legal compliance.
</Warning>

## Closing the Custody Chain

When all information is complete and verified:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Review All Fields">
    Ensure:
    - All dates and times are accurate
    - Volumes are correctly entered
    - All equipment is listed
    - Contact information is complete
    - PDF is uploaded
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Set Status to CLOSE">
    Click "Close" or change status to CLOSE.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="System Actions">
    When closed:
    - Status changes to CLOSE (Cerrado)
    - Cannot be deleted (preservation of legal record)
    - Can be edited if needed (before invoicing)
    - Becomes available for inclusion in work order sheet
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Editing Custody Chains

You can edit a custody chain if:
- Status is DRAFT or CLOSE
- Has not been included in an invoiced work order

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Custody Chain">
    Navigate to the custody chain in the work order sheet.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Click Edit">
    Modify any fields as needed.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Save Changes">
    Updates are tracked with modified timestamp.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  Even closed custody chains can be edited if necessary. However, once the work order is invoiced, the custody chain should not be modified to maintain billing integrity.
</Note>

## Custody Chain in Billing

### How Custody Chains Appear in Work Orders

When a custody chain is closed and the work order sheet is compiled:

1. **Service Line Items**: Created for the collection service
   - Description includes waste types collected
   - Quantity based on volume and hours
   - Cost based on project resource rates

2. **Equipment Line Items**: For each equipment detail
   - Links to project resource used
   - References custody chain consecutive number

3. **Volume Totals**: Aggregated in work order sheet
   - Sum of all custody chain volumes in the period
   - Displayed as total gallons, barrels, cubic meters

## Viewing and Tracking

### Custody Chain List

From the work order sheet view:
- See all custody chains for that sheet
- Filter by status (DRAFT, CLOSE, CANCELLED)
- Sort by date or consecutive number
- Quick view of volumes and dates

### Project Summary

From project dashboard:
- **Total Custody Chains**: Count across all work orders
- **Recent Activity**: Latest custody chain records
- **Volume Trends**: Totals over time

## Best Practices

### Documentation Best Practices

- **Immediate Recording**: Create custody chain same day as collection
- **Accurate Volumes**: Verify measurements from truck gauges
- **Complete Signatures**: Obtain all required signatures before closing
- **Clear Location**: Specify exact location within camp or site
- **Photo Evidence**: Keep photos with external records (not stored in system)

### Data Entry Best Practices

- **Consistent Naming**: Use standard equipment codes and descriptions
- **Time Accuracy**: Record actual start and end times for labor billing
- **Waste Type Selection**: Be specific about which waste types were collected
- **Volume Units**: Use gallons as primary unit, add others if contract requires

### Compliance Best Practices

- **Legal Requirements**: Ensure custody chains meet local regulatory requirements
- **Signature Authority**: Verify client contact has authority to sign
- **Document Retention**: Uploaded PDFs serve as legal records
- **Chain Integrity**: Never delete closed custody chains

## Common Scenarios

### Scenario 1: Standard Daily Collection

1. Arrive on site at 08:00
2. Begin collection from treatment plant
3. Collect 500 gallons of treated wastewater
4. Complete at 10:30 (2.5 hours)
5. Obtain client signature
6. Create custody chain with all details
7. Close and include in monthly work order

### Scenario 2: Emergency Grease Trap Service

1. Emergency call for grease trap overflow
2. Deploy vacuum truck and technician
3. Collect organic grease (special waste type)
4. Document emergency nature in equipment detail
5. Obtain customer acknowledgment
6. Create custody chain marked for expedited billing
7. Include in next work order for immediate invoicing

### Scenario 3: Multi-Equipment Collection

1. Service multiple treatment plants in one trip
2. Create one custody chain for the visit
3. Add separate equipment detail lines for each plant
4. Record combined volumes
5. Total time covers all collection activities
6. Single document covers entire route

## Consecutive Number Format

Custody chains use a global consecutive number:

**Format**: 7 digits, zero-padded

Examples:
- First custody chain: `0000001`
- Hundredth: `0000100`
- Thousandth: `0001000`

<Tip>
  The consecutive number is unique across all projects and never resets. This ensures each custody chain has a globally unique identifier.
</Tip>
